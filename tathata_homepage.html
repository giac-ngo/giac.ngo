English

I. On the Nature of Words

The Buddha taught that words are but fingers pointing at the moon. This library is filled with profound texts, yet not a single word is the Truth. The Truth is the silent "Suchness" that exists before the first letter was ever written.

II. On Reality as It Is

Tathata is the "Thusness" of the moment. It is the rain falling, the wind blowing, and the screen glowing â€” all without the labels we give them.

III. On the Purpose of Study

We preserve these teachings not to fill your head with concepts, but to help you empty it. Use these texts to realize that you are already what you are seeking.

IV. On the Digital Gate

This digital space is a mirror. If you come with a seeking mind, you will find more words. If you come with a quiet mind, you will find the Buddha.

  • When you see a flower and do not name it "flower," that is Suchness.
  • When you feel a thought and do not call it "mine," that is Suchness.
  • When you witness the world without wanting it to be different, that is Reality.

About Tathata Zen Monastery

Tathata, meaning "Suchness" or "Thusness," points to the ultimate nature of reality as it is, beyond concepts and descriptions. Our monastery is dedicated to the preservation and study of the Buddha's teachings across all traditions.

This digital library serves as a resource for practitioners and scholars alike, offering access to some of the most profound texts in Buddhist literature. May these teachings guide you on your path to awakening.
